WAMO Bot

AI Chatbot for Role-Based Training & Process Adoption · American Heart Association

image coming soon

The American Heart Association's WAMO program was a multi-year transformation of how the national Marketing and Communications department received, managed, and delivered work, spanning process redesign, Workfront for work management, and Bynder for digital asset management. Two years of role-specific training content existed across nine user personas, collected in a SharePoint knowledge base and Workfront and Bynder support portals. The gap wasn't content coverage: staff needed real-time assistance while completing day-to-day work in Workfront and Bynder, not a separate resource to go find.

The solution was WAMO Bot: a Power Virtual Agents chatbot built on Azure Bot Framework, deployed on Microsoft Teams, designed to provide real-time natural language support by routing users to role-specific resources on demand. A deliberate V1 scope constraint kept the build lean and fast to deploy: surface existing assets only, no new content required. Discovery research across nine personas confirmed that the gap was access, not coverage, making this constraint a product decision, not a limitation. The PRD defined 14 custom conversation topics mapped to three path types: internal SharePoint resources, external Workfront and Bynder support documentation, and human escalation via the WAMO support team.

User testing identified five critical gaps before launch: trigger phrase mismatches, persona routing ambiguity, conversation paths that were too deep, buried video content, and premature escalation before self-service options were exhausted. All five were resolved through targeted iteration on trigger logic, path depth, and escalation timing. Unanimous pilot authorization was secured from IT, MarComm Operations, and Program Leadership with no unresolved blockers.
